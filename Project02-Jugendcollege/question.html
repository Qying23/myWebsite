Admission to the Youth College is possible via two tracks: Recognized or beneficially entitled adolescents are credited for clearing via their caregiver at the AMS Vienna. Persons in the asylum procedure, who are in basic care, come to the education hub through the Social Welfare Fund. After a consultation in the educational hub, young people can be booked to the Youth College. Even privately accommodated young people can contact the educational hub: bildungsdrehscheibe@vhs.at

Courses offered at the Youth College include the core modules Basic Education (Mathematics, English, ICT) and German Modules; Special modules, which are combined depending on their level, prior knowledge and educational or vocational educational goals (eg compulsory schooling, peer interpreting and workshops) and module-related offers, such as socially integrative activities, educational and career counseling and support services. The average length of stay in the youth college is 9 months, the timetable for the participants is always 8 weeks. This structure allows flexibility and individualization of training. The goal of the youth college is the placement in a secondary school, a vocational training or in a sustainable employment.

Six million euros are spent on this educational project each year, half of which is funded by the European Social Fund (ESF), the other half by the Department for Integration and Diversity MA 17, AMS Vienna and FSW Vienna. A bidding consortium consisting of nine partners (Wiener Volkshochschulen GmbH, WUK Association for the Creation of Open Culture and Workshop Houses, Caritas, Integrationshaus, Vienna Interface, abz * austria, equalizent, PROSA, BPI of ÖJAB) with the VHS Vienna as Lead Partner is leading the Youth college at the two locations. The offer started in late summer and will be rolled out in full operation on 1 October.
